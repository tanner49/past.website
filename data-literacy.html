Introduction - Vocabulary

Take a few minutes and read through the following terms and definitions. These terms will be used throughout your activities today and you may want to refer back to this list.

  • statistical question -one that can be answered by collecting data and where there will be variability in the data. a question that you may get several possible answers
  • interpret - describe what you found out, explain
  • data - a representation of a fact or figure. data can be the basis of graphs or observations
  • numerical data set – a set of data which consists of digits
  • quantitative measures - a measurement that is related to a number or quantity
  • mean - the average of a data set found by finding the sum and dividing by the number of addends
  • median-the middle number of an ordered data set or the average of the two middle numbers in an ordered data set with an even number of data
  • range - the difference between the greatest number and the least number in a data set
  • interquartile range - the difference between the lower 25% and the lower 75%, the range the middle 50% lies.
  • data distribution - an arrangement of values showing their frequency of occurrence

Examples

To the right are the prices of 2 well know stocks for the last 9 days. Below are four example questions like those you will need to answer in this assignment.

1: What are the number of observations given for Apple's closing prices?

Answer: 9 - The number of observations for this data set is 9. 9 prices are given.

2: What is the range of Coke’s closing prices?

$27. The highest value – lowest value is the range, $60-$33 = $27

3: What is the average (mean) value of Apple for the days given?

The mean is the sum of all the prices divided by the total number of observations. To find the mean:

  1. Sum all the prices: $322 + $350 + $339 + $357 +$333 + $351 + $327 + $350 +$334 = $3063
  2. Divide by the number of observations: $3063 divided by 9 = $340.33. This is the mean price of apple stock.

4: What is the median value for Coke for the given days?

Answer:The median is the center value in the data set. Since we have nine values, it is the fifth highest value. To find the median value:

  1. Order all of your data from smallest to largest: $33, $37, $42, $43, $43, $44, $46, $48, $60
  2. Select the middle value (in this case, the ffith value) in this list. That value is $43.
